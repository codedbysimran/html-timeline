Timeline of major festivals celebrated in India

January- Makar Sankranti

celebrated across different states,marking the conclusion of long winter nights

February- Vasant Panchami

dedicated to goddess saraswati,the goddess of wisdom,education,art and culture.

March- Holi

The festival of colors.

April- Ugadi

The Telugu and Kannada New Year.

May- Eid al-fitr

Celebrates the end of Ramadan, the Islamic holy month of fasting.

June- Eid al-adha

This Islamic festival, also known as Bakrid, is a celebration of sacrifice and faith

July- Guru Purnima

celebrated to honour one's chosen spiritual teachers or leaders

August- Raksha Bandhan

Celebrates the bond between brothers and sisters.

September- Ganesh chaturthi

Celebrates the birth of Lord Ganesha, especially significant in Maharashtra.

October- Dussehra

Celebrates the victory of good over evil, marking the culmination of Navratri.

November- Diwali

The festival of lights, symbolizing the victory of light over darkness and good over evil, celebrated by Hindus, Sikhs, and Jains.

December- Christmas

Celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ, widely celebrated across India.
